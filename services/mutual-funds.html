--- layout: default title: Mutual Fund Advisory permalink: /services/mutual-funds/ faqs: - question: "What is the minimum amount to start investing?" answer: "You can start a Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) with as little as ₹500 or ₹1,000 per month. Lumpsum investments typically require a minimum of ₹5,000. We recommend an amount based on your overall financial goals." - question: "How long is my money locked in?" answer: "Most open-ended mutual funds are highly liquid, meaning you can withdraw your money at any time. The only major exception is ELSS (Equity Linked Savings Scheme) tax-saving funds, which have a mandatory lock-in period of 3 years." - question: "How does Earthen Capital select the right funds?" answer: "We don't just chase past performance. We evaluate the fund manager's conviction, rolling returns across different market cycles, downside protection (Sortino ratio), and the fund's adherence to its stated investment mandate." - question: "Are mutual fund returns taxable?" answer: "Yes, taxation depends on the type of fund (equity vs. debt) and your holding period. Equity funds held for over a year are subject to Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG) tax, while debt fund taxation is aligned with your income tax slab. We help structure your investments to optimize for tax efficiency." - question: "Can I stop or pause my SIP if I face a financial emergency?" answer: "Absolutely. SIPs are completely flexible. You can pause, stop, or decrease your SIP amount at any time without any penalties from the AMC. You maintain total control over your cash flow." ---
