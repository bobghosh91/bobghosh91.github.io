                <div class="p-5 rounded-lg border border-gray-200 dark:border-gray-700 hover:shadow-lg transition-shadow dark:bg-gray-800">

                    <p class="text-sm mt-2 text-gray-600 dark:text-gray-300">Owning a share in a business. High risk, High return (11-13%). Best for long-term goals like Kids' Education or Retirement.</p>

                    <p class="text-sm mt-2 text-gray-600 dark:text-gray-300">Lending money (like FD, PPF). Safe and stable, but lower returns (6-8%). Good for short-term needs.</p>

                    <p class="text-sm mt-2 text-gray-600 dark:text-gray-300">Safety net against inflation. Better to buy <strong>Gold Bonds (SGB) or ETFs</strong> rather than jewelry to save on making charges.</p>

                    <p class="text-sm mt-2 text-gray-600 dark:text-gray-300">Physical property. Needs lots of cash to start, hard to sell quickly. Rental income in India is actually quite low (2-3%).</p>

            <h3 class="text-2xl font-semibold text-gray-800 dark:text-gray-100 mb-4">Mutual Funds: The Beginner's Best Friend</h3>
            <p class="mb-4">
                For 99% of us, picking individual stocks is risky gambling. <strong>Mutual Funds</strong> let you pool money with millions of others to invest safely.
            </p>
            <ul class="list-disc pl-6 mb-6 space-y-3">
                <li><strong>Index Funds (Passive):</strong> These just copy a market list (like Nifty 50). Low fees, less headache, and usually give good returns over time.</li>
                <li><strong>Active Mutual Funds:</strong> A fund manager tries to beat the market by picking "winning" stocks. They charge higher fees (Expense Ratio).</li>
                <li><strong>SIP (Systematic Investment Plan):</strong> This isn't a product, it's a <em>habit</em>. You invest a fixed amount (e.g., â‚¹2,000) every month automatically. It brings discipline to your life.</li>
