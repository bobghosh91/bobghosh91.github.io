            <h3 class="text-2xl font-semibold text-gray-800 dark:text-gray-100 mb-4">Mutual Funds: The Beginner's Power Tool</h3>
            <p class="mb-4">
                For 99% of beginners, picking stocks is risky. <strong>Mutual Funds</strong> allow you to pool money with millions of others.
            </p>
            <ul class="list-disc pl-6 mb-6 space-y-3">
                <li><strong>Index Funds (Passive):</strong> These simply copy a market index (like Nifty 50). They have very low fees and usually beat active funds over the long term.</li>
                <li><strong>Active Mutual Funds::</strong>A manager tries to beat the market by picking "winning" stocks. Higher fees (Expense Ratio).</li>
                <li><strong>SIP (Systematic Investment Plan):</strong> This is not a product, but a <em>method</em>. You invest a fixed amount (e.g., â‚¹2,000) every month. It automates discipline and averages out market ups and downs.</li>
